2.1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

People who are hard of hearing or deaf require such things as sign language or written words to communicate. This disability, to some extent, and the use of the internet are a great match. The vast majority of website content is written and or with pictures. There are instances like with video where the disability would need wording with subtitles or a signer present or on screen to interpret the audio. This is where there are a lot of issues unfortunately, but there are websites such as YouTube that have subtitled enabled videos, this is a great step forward for accessibility for partial and deaf users. For people that are blind how ever there are ways to combat the restraints of using the internet. From using coding to help describe content such as pictures which then programs help the user hear the structural content via dictation.[1]

[1] - https://www.w3.org/WAI/people-use-web/user-stories/
