2.1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright is an incredible thing, it gives the owner of something like a piece of art protection from others that could potentially use the said piece of art for their own material gain. It gives the owner basically the freedom to do whatever they want, from selling the copyright to giving it to someone for inheritance.[1]

“Websites contain written material, graphics, business logos, and other visual and sound elements. This firmly categorizes them as copyrightable material.” [2]

When I post a picture online, I don’t want someone else to steal it and use it for their own gain. To combat this, I have settings in my website to prevent any theft, but there are ways and means, as always it seems, to combat this. The website I use to upload my images allows me to set restrictions to only have the picture viewed in a low resolution which means someone can’t reproduce the image to its full potential if they decide to steal it from the site by a screen grab or right clicking and saving the picture.

[1] - https://www.whoishostingthis.com/resources/copyright-guide/

[2] - https://www.termsfeed.com/blog/website-copyright-law/
